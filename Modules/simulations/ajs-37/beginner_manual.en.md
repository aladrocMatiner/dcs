### The 6 Things You Always Monitor

1. **Airspeed** (km/h): are you fast enough to take off / climb / land?
2. **Altitude** (m): are you climbing/descending as expected?
3. **Attitude** (pitch/roll): are you level? are you over-rotating?
4. **Engine**: RPM / EGT / EPR (do you have thrust, are temperatures normal?)
5. **Warnings**: master caution, warning lights.
6. **HUD** (if used): takeoff symbology and flight path vector.

## 3) Taxi (First-Time Friendly)

Taxi is where many beginners crash. Go slow.

- The engine produces a lot of thrust at idle; use small throttle inputs.
- Steering: use pedals; tighter turns require differential braking.
- If you use thrust reverser while taxiing: ensure the area behind is clear.

Goal: reach the runway without overheating, overspeeding, or striking objects.

## 4) Takeoff (Beginner Method)

Use the HUD method from the official procedure.

Before you roll:

- Align with runway.
- Set `Master Mode` to `NAV` **at least 2 minutes** before takeoff power.
- Set landing light `LANDNING` ON.

Procedure (simplified):

1. Hold wheel brakes.
2. Advance throttle to max power **without afterburner**.
3. Release brakes, keep centerline with pedals.
4. If runway is short/heavy: ignite afterburner.
5. Rotate at the correct cues (HUD markers / time-distance line).
6. When airborne: gear up.

Reference: see [quick_takeoff.en.md](quick_takeoff.en.md) for the full checklist and the manual figures.
